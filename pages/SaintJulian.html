Here is everything about Saint Julian's that we can visit.

Spinola Bay

Spinola Bay is a picturesque area in St. Julian’s, characterized by its traditional Maltese fishing boats and lined with excellent restaurants and cafes.

Portomaso Marina

Portomaso Marina is a luxurious and modern yacht marina surrounded by a complex of upscale apartments, hotels, and a variety of restaurants.

St. George’s Beach

St. George’s Beach is a popular sandy beach in St. Julian’s, known for its clear waters and vibrant atmosphere. It’s a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

San Anton Palace

San Anton Palace, an iconic landmark of Malta, commands attention in the the heart of Attard. San Anton Palace was established in 1625. Game of Thrones was taken up there.

St Julian's Tower

Saint Julian's Tower, originally known as Torre di San Giuliano and also known as Sliema Tower, is a small watchtower in Sliema, Malta. It was completed in 1658 as the fifth of the De Redin towers. An artillery battery was built around the tower in 1715. Today, the tower and the remains of the battery are a restaurant.

Balluta Bay - A Sliema hot spot for sunbathing and café-hopping

Balluta Bay on the northern edge of Sliema lets the clear waters of the Med roll over white-tinted pebbles. On one side, the Gothic filigrees of the Carmelite Parish Church draw the eye. Behind is a bustling piazza where you can sit with a coffee and watch the world go by. During the summer months, Balluta Bay is a favourite local swimming spot. More of a classic beach than the rocky headlands elsewhere in the town, it's got space for laying the towel and soaking up the rays. You might want to bring the snorkel along, too – there are some interesting fish-filled reefs lining the bay.
