Here is everything about Sliema that we can visit.

Grandmaster's Palace

The Grandmaster's Palace is a palace in Valletta, Malta. It was built between the 16th and 18th centuries as the palace of the Grand Master of the Order of St. John, who ruled Malta from 1530 to 1798. The palace became a British royal residence with various governors representing the monarchy and the queen, and with the royal family itself. As an official royal residence it was often used by British sovereigns for major events during their stay in Malta.

St. John's Co-Cathedral

St. John's Co-Cathedral is a Catholic co-cathedral in Valletta, Malta, dedicated to Saint John the Baptist. It was built by the Order of St. John between 1573 and 1578. The church was designed by the Maltese architect Girolamo Cassar, who designed several of the more prominent buildings in Valletta.

Upper Barrakka Gardens

Upper Barrakka Gardens are a public garden in Valletta, Malta. Along with the Lower Barrakka Gardens in the same city, they offer a panoramic view of the Grand Harbour. The gardens are located on the upper tier of Saints Peter and Paul Bastion, which was built in the 1560s. The bastion's lower tier contains the Saluting Battery. The garden's terraced arches were built in 1661 by the Italian knight Fra Flaminio Balbiani. They were originally roofed, but the ceiling was removed following the Rising of the Priests in 1775.

Valletta Waterfront

The Valletta Waterfront was once a rundown dockside area, now renovated and lined with waterside restaurants. Most of its services, including shops, restaurants and bars, That cater to the passengers of the cruise ships that dock here. There's a small tourist information booth, plus operators offering bus and boat trips around Valletta.

San Anton Gardens

San Anton Gardens are located in Attard and is one of the most beautiful gardens in Malta with a large variety of beautiful flowers and plants. This garden has been open to the public since 1882 and it was built by Grand Master Antoine de Paule to complement his summer residence, San Anton Palace, which today is the residence of the Maltese President.

St. Lucia Street, Il-Belt

St. Lucia Street is a street that has a family-run restauranta that are located in the heart of Valletta.

Republic Street

Republic Street is a lovely pedestrian street that runs through the centre of Valletta Malta. The Valletta Bus Terminus, City Gates and Triton Fountain are located at the western end of the street while Fort Elmo is at the eastern end. Visitors to Malta should definitely include a stroll along Republic Street in their itineraries. It is the primary shopping street in Valletta.

Tritons' Fountain

Tritons' Fountain is a fountain located in Floriana, Malta. It consists of three bronze Tritons holding up a large basin, balanced on a concentric base built out of concrete and clad in 730 tons of travertine slabs. The fountain is one of Malta's most important Modernist landmarks.

Cannon, Saluting Battery

The Saluting Battery is located on one of the best vantage points overlooking the Grand Harbour. Its origins are as old as those of the City itself. The Saluting Battery is where a cannon once fired salutes to visiting naval vessels. The battery has been restored, and a cannon is fired Monday through Saturday at noon and 4pm with great ceremony
